featured places

The Grand Egyptian Museum

The Grand Egyptian Museum or is located a few miles west of Cairo near the Pyramids of Giza. It is being built to be the world's largest archaeological museum, accommodating 5 million visitors annually.

Sharm El-Shaikh

Sharm El Sheikh is an Egyptian tourist city, located at the confluence of the Gulfs of Aqaba and Suez on the Red Sea coast. It covers an area of 480 km, has a population of 35,000, and is the largest city in the South Sinai Governorate.

Hurghada

Hurghada is the administrative capital of the Red Sea Governorate, and it occupies an area of 40 km of the coastal strip on the western shore of the Red Sea.

The Road Of Rams

The Way of Rams is a 3,500-year-old road that is 2.7 miles long and about 250 feet wide, connecting the Temple of Karnak and Luxor Temple. It was discovered in the ancient city of Thebes, where sphinxes and ram-headed statues are lined up on both sides.

Nuba

The country of Nubia is the historical region that extends along the Nile from the first cataract south of Aswan in the north to the south of the confluence of the Blue and White Niles in the south.

New Library of Alexandria

The New Library of Alexandria or the General Authority of the Library of Alexandria is a revival of the ancient Library of Alexandria, the largest library of its era, in a huge project undertaken by Egypt in partnership with the United Nations.
